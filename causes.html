IT'S GETTING TOO DANG HOT

How exactly did global warming start occuring, and why should you be worried about it?

Causes

Global warming results from increased greenhouse gases in the atmosphere.

Main contributors: CO2, methane, nitrous oxide emissions.

Causes include burning fossil fuels, deforestation, industrial processes.

CO2 released from energy, transportation; deforestation reduces carbon sinks.

Methane from livestock, rice paddies, landfills, fossil fuels; potent heat-trapper.

Nitrous oxide from agriculture, industrial activities; strong greenhouse gas.

Deforestation reduces carbon absorption by trees

Industrial processes and waste release greenhouse gases.

Land use changes and permafrost thaw also release gases

Consequences: rising temperatures, sea levels, extreme weather, ecosystem disruption.

Use of Synthetic Chemicals: Some synthetic chemicals used in industrial processes and products, such as hydrofluorocarbons (HFCs), perfluorocarbons (PFCs), and sulfur hexafluoride (SF6), have extremely high global warming potential

Solutions: reduce emissions, transition to renewables, sustainable practices.
